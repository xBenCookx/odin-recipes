Meatball Baked Ziti

This dish is perfect for a quick homemade dinner that will please everyone!

Ingredients

  • 1 pound ziti pasta
  • 3/4 pound ground beef
  • 3/4 pound ground pork
  • 1/3 cup Panko
  • 2 large egg yolks
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1/2 teaspoon dried basil
  • 1/2 teaspoon dried oregano
  • Kosher salt and black pepper
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1/3 cup dry red wine
  • 32 ounce jar of marinara sauce
  • 3 tablespoons of chopped fresh basil
  • 3 tablespoons of chopped fresh parsley leaves
  • 8 ounces of burrata cheese, torn into large pieces

Instructions

  1. Preheat the oven to 375 degrees fahrenheit. Lightly oil a 9 x 13 baking dish or coat with nonstick spray.
  2. In a large pot of boiling water with added salt, cook the pasta according to the package instructions and then drain well.
  3. In a large bowl, combine the ground beef, pork, Panko, egg yolks, garlic, basil and oregano. Season with 1 teaspoon salt and 1/2 teaspoon of pepper. Using a spoon and clean hands, stir until well combined. Roll this mixture into 1 to 1 1/4 inch meatballs, forming about 24 of them.
  4. Heat olive oil in a dutch oven over medium high heat. Add meatballs, in batches, and cook until all sides are browned, about 2-3 minutes. Return meatballs to the dutch oven.
  5. Stir in wine, scraping any browned bits from the bottom of the dutch oven.
  6. Stir in marinara sauce and bring to a boil. Reduce the heat and simmer until the flavors have blended, which taked about 5 minutes. Remove from heat; stir in pasta, basil and parsley.
  7. Spread pasta mixture into the prepared baking dish; top with burrata.
  8. Place into oven and bake until bubbly, which takes about 25 to 30 minutes. Let it sit for about 10 minutes.
  9. Serve and enjoy!
