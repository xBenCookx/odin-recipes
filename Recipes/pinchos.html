Pinchos con Pollos

Pinchos is a puerto rican dish that translates to "spikes" or "skewers". They are a great snack to have with drinks. Pinchos are usually made with chicken, but you can find them with vegetables, beef, or pork as well. These skewers usually also come with a piece of bread on the end of the skewer.

Here are the ingredients and instructions for my all time favorite drink snack!

Ingredients

  • Chicken Thighs - These are great due to the amount of fat that is in them to withstand grilling without drying out. You could also use beef, pork, or vegetables if you wanted as well! 3lbs, or around 5 to 6 chicken thighs, will do the trick.
  • Abodo and Sazòn - These spices are a puerto rican blend, and adds some flavor to infuse on your chicken. 2 tsp of abodo and 1 1/2 tsp of sazòn is all you need.
  • 2 tsp of salt - I do not think there is much info needed here.
  • Cooking Oil - around 2 Tbsp of oil, unless you would like a tad more for greasing.
  • BBQ Sauce - You can use any BBQ sauce you want for this. However, I would stay away from something too sweet. About one cup will work great.
  • Bread - Last but not least, bread! I personally prefer sourdough, but this is totally up to you.

Instruction

  1. Clean the chickens dry with a paper towel. Make sure to chop the thighs into bite-size pieces.
  2. Add the abodo, sazòn, and salt to a large bowl and mix them up.
  3. Now it is time to add the chopped pieces of chicken to the bowl with the cooking oil and mix until the chicken bits are completely covered. Cover with plastic wrap or parchment directly on the surface of the thighs and marinate for at least an hour, but I would say overnight.
  4. Around 30 minutes before you pull the chicken out, soak the skewers in water so they dont burn.
  5. Preheat the grill on high, then add around 5 to 6 chicken chunks to thke skewers. Make sure they are tight, but not crowding each other. Brush both sides of each spikes with BBQ sauce, about 1/2 cup in total.
  6. When the grill pan is hot, brush it with a touch of oil. Then place the skewers on the grill pan, making sure not to crowd the pan or place them too close to each other.
  7. After around five minutes, brush 1/4 cup of BBQ sauce on the top curface of the chicken. Next, flip the chicken, and brush another 1/4 cup of BBQ sauce on the top layer. Cook them for about five more minutes, until char develops and the internal temp reaches 160 degres farhenheit.
  8. All that is left is to take them off the grill and slide a piece of bread on top.

Now it is time to snack on! Enjoy!
